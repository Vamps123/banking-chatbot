Personal loans allow customers to borrow funds for personal needs such as home repairs, education, and medical expenses. Interest rates for personal loans typically range between 10.5% and 16.5% depending on credit score and tenure. Loan amounts can range from $2,500 to $50,000 with terms between 12 and 60 months.

Credit cards provide a revolving credit line. The interest rate on credit card balances is usually expressed as an APR. Many banks offer a 0% introductory APR for up to 12 months on purchases, with a standard APR between 18.9% and 24.9% after the introductory period. Late payment fees are usually between $25 and $40.

Savings accounts earn interest on deposits. Standard savings account annual percentage yields (APY) range from 0.01% to 0.25% for basic accounts. High-yield savings accounts can offer up to 4.5% APY. Most banks require a minimum balance of $100 to avoid monthly maintenance fees.

Checking accounts are designed for daily transactions and generally include debit cards, online bill pay, and direct deposit. Some checking accounts offer rewards for debit card spending. Overdraft protection and monthly maintenance fees depend on account type and relationship with the bank.

Mortgage loans are secured loans used to purchase real estate. Fixed-rate mortgages provide stable monthly payments over the loan term, usually 15 or 30 years. Adjustable-rate mortgages (ARMs) can change after the initial fixed period, and mortgage insurance may be required for down payments under 20%.

Banking security best practices include enabling two-factor authentication, reviewing account activity frequently, and not sharing one-time passwords or PINs. Customers should report lost or stolen debit or credit cards immediately to the bank.
